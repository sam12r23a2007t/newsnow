Twitter denies hacking, says no evidence data up for sale is from its system
Twitter has denied that it was hacked and the data being sold online was stolen from its systems. Last week, Alon Gal, co-founder of Israeli cybersecurity-monitoring firm Hudson Rock posted on LinkedIn that he had discovered stolen data, which contained email addresses of more than 200 million Twitter users. Gal wrote that the data was posted on an online hacking forum and that the breach would likely lead to “hacking, targeted phishing and doxxing,” and termed it a ‘significant leak.’ He said that Twitter had not responded to him when he had alerted the company about the same.

WazirX declares assets via Proof-of-Reserves, more than 90% of users' funds in Binance wallets
One of India's major crypto exchanges, WazirX, has disclosed its assets via the Proof-of-Reserves(POR) report and it states that 90 percent of the users' funds are in Binance wallets. CoinGabbar, which is an Indian third-party crypto assets platform, released the WazirX POR report. According to the report, at the time of writing this article, WazirX holds $284.64 million in USDT, which is pegged to the US Dollar. Of the total, almost 90 percent of the users' funds were found to be in Binance wallets while the remaining 10 percent(almost $26.5 million) was being held in other exchanges.
